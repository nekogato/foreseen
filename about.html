(About 簡介)

《 FORESEEN 2099》是一個前衛的展覽，探討藝術、科技與文化遺產保存的融合。該計畫延續了預見地產機構的開創性努力，在 2099 年的推測時間軸中，突顯了香港傳統小企業的寶貴遺產。透過一項雄心勃勃的數位檔案計劃，這項藝術工作將參觀者推向 75 年後的未來，迫使他們重新評估荃灣和新蒲崗社區的當今實踐和未來。

與南豐集團的 "與時俱進 "計劃合作，《 FORESEEN 2099》與社區可持續發展和文化延續的願望產生了深刻共鳴。被選中的行業--家用電器維修、服裝改製和珠寶/金屬製品--體現了一種堅韌不拔、足智多謀的持久精神，延長了日常用品的生命週期。這些行業讓我們想起了那個時代，在那個時代，人們更傾向於修理而不是丟棄，體現了永續發展的精神。

《 FORESEEN 2099》》的旅程分為三個精心製作的階段： 在南風的主持下，每個階段都在獨特的場地舉行。

《啟動》階段邀請觀眾從2099 年穿越到2024 年。在這裡，南豐的兩台卡片機充當了傳送門的角色 — 投幣式設備發放嵌入AR 代碼的插圖卡片。這些插圖作為"時間鑰匙"，透過Instagram 開啟了一條增強現實隧道，使觀眾能夠虛擬地回到2024 年，點燃了他們的好奇心，並促使他們探索這些相關的實體商店。

在 The Mills 舉辦的 《探測》(Survey）活動從數位領域發展到有形的接觸。觀眾利用他們的數位設備來 "挖掘 "商店的遺跡，將這些舉足輕重的小企業的虛擬和實體故事連接起來。這一階段向參與者提出了挑戰，要求他們挖掘出這些如今已不為人知但卻具有豐富歷史內涵的實體的敘事層次。

在 AIRSIDE 的《挖掘和保存》(Excavation and Preseravtion)將展覽推向高潮，以身臨其境的方式慶祝研究之旅。壓軸展品包括精心策劃的研究圖、推測漫畫、3D雕塑和一個互動裝置 — 挖掘機。在這裡，2台爪式機器變成了檔案挖掘現場。觀眾親自動手，提取 3D 列印的人工製品和掃描的小型企業物品的遺物。這種互動體驗與考古探索相呼應，並附有證書，詳細說明每件文物的出處，從而鞏固了從過去到未來的聯繫。

《 FORESEEN 2099》挑釁性地將觀眾置於一個雙面刃的角色中 — 在投機資本主義框架內，遊走於保護與消費之間。透過將參觀者定位為數位"計劃外"房產的潛在消費者（一旦實體店消失，這些房產就會成為他們的），展覽迫使人們反思在城市不斷發展的同時，保護文化遺產的共謀性和緊迫性。

歸根究底，《FORESEEN 2099》不僅是致敬，更是向藝術家、檔案保管員、文化工作者、學者、開發商和更廣泛的社區發出的號角。透過採用帶有諷刺意味的資本主義語言，我們強調了傳統小企業不可取代的價值。這是一項具有遠見卓識的工作--想像出新的經濟模式，以確保這些文化基石的可持續性，同時點燃社區奉獻和遺產保護的集體實踐。

FORESEEN 2099 is an avant-garde exhibition that probes the fusion of art, technology, and cultural heritage preservation. A continuation of the pioneering efforts of the Foreseen Property Agency, this project foregrounds the invaluable legacies of Hong Kong's traditional small businesses within the speculative timeline of the year 2099. Through an ambitious digital archive initiative, this artistic endeavor thrusts visitors forward 75 years into the future, compelling them to reassess present-day practices and futures of communities in Tsuen Wan and San Po Kong.

In collaboration with the Nan Fung Group’s "In Time Of" initiative, Foreseen 2099 resonates deeply with aspirations for community sustainability and cultural continuity. The selected industries—household appliance repair, clothing alteration, and jewelry/metal works—embody a durable spirit of resilience and resourcefulness, extending the lifecycle of everyday objects. These industries remind us of an era where repair was preferred over disposal, encapsulating the ethos of sustainability.

The journey of FORESEEN 2099 unfolds in three meticulously crafted stages: Inspection, Excavation, and Preservation, each hosted at unique venues under the aegis of Nan Fung.

Switch On, invites the audience on a captivating time-travel experience from 2099 to 2024. Here, two card machines in the Nan Fung Palace serve as portals – coin-operated devices dispensing illustrated cards embedded with AR codes. These illustrations, acting as “time keys,” unlock an augmented reality tunnel via Instagram, enabling audiences to virtually travel back to 2024, igniting curiosity and driving them to explore these relevant stores physically.

Survey, hosted at The Mills, evolves from the digital domain into tangible encounters. Audience members utilize their digital devices to metaphorically “excavate” the store remnants, bridging virtual and physical stories of these pivotal small businesses. This stage challenges participants to unearth the narrative layers of these now-obscure yet historically rich entities.

Excavation and Preservation at AIRSIDE culminates the exhibition with an immersive celebration of the research journey. This finale features a curated constellation of research drawings, speculative videos, sculptures, and an epitomic installation—*Excavator*. Here, four claw machines transform into archival dig sites. Audience members engage hands-on, extracting 3D printed artifacts, relics of the scanned small business objects. This interactive experience, echoing archaeological exploration, comes augmented with certificates detailing the provenance of each artifact, thus cementing the link from past to future.

FORESEEN 2099 provocatively enmeshes the audience in a double-edged role—one that navigates the tension between preservation and consumption within a speculative capitalist framework. By situating visitors as potential consumers of digital "off-plan" properties that become theirs once the real stores vanish, the exhibition forces a reflection on one's complicity and urgency in preserving cultural heritage amidst unrelenting urban growth.

Ultimately, FORESEEN 2099 isn't just a homage; it is a clarion call to artists, archivists, cultural workers, academics, developers, and the broader community. By adopting the languages of capitalism laced with sarcasm, we foreground the irreplaceable value of traditional small businesses. It is a visionary endeavor—one that imagines new economic models to ensure the sustainability of these cultural keystones, while igniting a collective practice of community dedication and heritage conservation.
