良記排檔百貨

1982年至現今

新蒲崗衍慶街 60號

受訪者：范桂永先生

業務性質：雜貨 修鞋 修理

范伯的店鋪包羅萬有，有龜糧、字畫、雀仔，還進行補鞋和賣珠寶。他十分強調所有東西都是自學的，以前甚至可以整雙鞋出來。每次去找他，他總是靜靜地在修鞋，似乎從未見過他停手。他說年輕時曾經跟團去澳門寫生，但搞藝術需要有家底，所以他最後只能把畫畫當作興趣，無師自通，和做鞋一樣，他覺得不懂就要學。 第一次見他時，他送了一幅其他人畫他店鋪的寫生給我。第二次見他，他送了一本他自己收藏的寫生集給我。

根記：梁生說，他退休後才開始轉行做補鞋，一做就是二十幾年。平時閒下來時，他最喜歡佈置店鋪、畫畫和做設計。他每週都會做運動，和老友一起打乒乓球、游泳。他說一看我的手指就知道我是藝術家，因為我手指又細又長，和他一樣。記得臨走的時候，我客氣地祝他身體健康、生意興隆。梁生馬上說：“不用生意興隆，千祈唔好這麼多生意。”的確，過著平靜的生活，夠就好了。

Leung Kee Pai Dong

1982 to present

60 Yin Hing St, San Po Kong

Interviewee: Mr. Fan kwai Wing

Business: Groceries, Shoe Repair, Repair

Leung Kee Pai Dong Store: Fan Bo's store is a one-stop shop, with tortoise food, paintings, and birds. He also does shoe repair and sells jewelry. He strongly emphasizes that everything he knows is self-taught - he used to even be able to make a whole pair of shoes. Whenever you go to see him, he's always quietly repairing shoes, seemingly never stopping. He says that in his younger days he used to go to Macau with a group to sketch, but pursuing art requires financial means, so in the end he could only treat painting as a hobby, being self-taught. Just like shoemaking, he feels that if you don't know how to do something, you have to learn.

The first time I met him, he gave me a painting of his store that someone else had done. The second time, he gave me a collection of his own sketches that he had compiled. Liang Sheng says that he only started doing shoe repair after retiring, and has been at it for over 20 years now. In his free time, he most enjoys decorating the store, painting, and doing design work. He exercises every week, playing ping pong and swimming with old friends. He said he could tell I was an artist just by looking at my fingers, because they're long and slim, just like his. As I was leaving, I politely wished him good health and prosperous business. Liang Sheng immediately said, "You don't need to wish for prosperous business, just don't let there be too much business." Indeed, living a peaceful life is enough.

Brown White
誠信補鞋配匙

1997年至現今

新蒲崗康景商場27號D鋪

受訪者：羅沛朝先生

業務性質：修鞋 配鎖匙

誠信最特別的，是它座落在康景樓裡。康景樓是香港iconic的一批「怪獸大廈」的表表者。

雖然大家都叫「怪獸大廈」，但它們「怪獸」之處各異其趣，新蒲崗的康景樓，在於它像由多件不同大塊積木般的組件、拼合成龐然巨廈。遠處望去甚有「霸氣」，難望清其全貌。

1972年落成的康景樓，在這座城市流行有大型「基座」的多層多棟私人住宅之前，就已經有特大的基座，多層停車場、壁畫和商場一應俱全。特別是，這樣的「舊私樓商場」，許多都「死」了，十室九空的多，唯獨是康景商場經營有道，成了住宅老商場的典範。大家想也沒想過，有天，康景大廈竟在「票選百大奇建築」的活動中名列前茅，被保存下來成為新蒲崗的地標。

在它被視作新地標時，康景已不再有人居住，但康景商場連同在其之上的所有樓層，被打造成全新的「垂直老派購物路線」。許多外面找不到的行業，全港碩果僅存的一間，都在此開業，誠信於是成了這兒的重點商號。幾百間這樣的「老派經典小店」，就只有誠信補鞋配匙是「原裝」的，在旁邊開業的店家都難以置信，經常來誠信聽老闆講往昔的新蒲崗故事。老闆最喜歡講的是：補鞋還補鞋，配匙還配匙。它們是幾時連在一起的呢？說著說著，就會講到康強街的街口曾有一檔補鞋、有一檔配匙，在街上日夕相對，就決定合併了。

老闆喜歡說，但凡有兩種技能不知何故拼在一起的，都有這種街頭上的因緣：生存艱難，後來經常見到的雪糕撈麵店、找換修甲店，都是這樣來的。在康景裡面，盡是這種文化研究者喜歡研究的「合併鋪」，看似自古已連在一起，但只有補鞋和配匙，才是最classic的。誠信補鞋配匙，竟因此成了無數大學生來做功課做訪問的研究對象。畢竟，實體鎖匙近年已無人再用，誠信幾乎被視作一間博物館，撐起了康景大廈的新文化經濟。

Shing Shun Shoes and Keys

1997 to present

Shop 27D, Hong King Building shopping centre, 86 Tseuk Luk St, San Po Kong

Business: Shoe Repair, Key Duplication

Interviewee: Mr. Brian Law

The most special thing about Cheng Shun is that it is located inside the Kwong King Building. The Kwong King Building is a representative of the iconic "monster buildings" in Hong Kong.

Although everyone calls them "monster buildings", they each have their own unique "monstrous" charm. For the Kwong King Building in San Po Kong, it is that it looks like a massive building assembled from different large blocks of components. Viewed from a distance, it has an imposing "domineering" presence, making it difficult to take in the entire structure.

The Kwong King Building, completed in 1972, was ahead of the trend of large-scale "podium" multi-story and multi-block private residential complexes in the city. It already had a massive podium with multi-story parking, murals, and a shopping mall. Interestingly, many of these "old private building shopping malls" have since "died", with most of them empty. But the Kwong King Mall has been well-operated, becoming a model for old residential building malls. No one ever imagined that one day, the Kwong King Building would be ranked among the top in a "100 Most Extraordinary Buildings" poll, and be preserved as a landmark in San Po Kong.

When it was seen as a new landmark, the Kwong King Building no longer had any residential tenants. But the Kwong King Mall, along with all the floors above it, have been transformed into a brand-new "vertical retro shopping route". Many industries that cannot be found elsewhere in the city, the sole survivors in Hong Kong, have set up shop here. Cheng Shun, a shoe repair and key duplication shop, has become a highlight of this mall. Among the hundreds of these "old-school classic small shops", Cheng Shun is the only one that is the "original" shoe repair and key duplication store. The neighboring shops find it hard to believe, and often come to Cheng Shun to listen to the owner tell stories of the old San Po Kong. The owner's favorite stories are about how they continue to do shoe repairs and key duplication, and how the two businesses came to be combined.

He would say that whenever there are two skills that are somehow combined together, it often has its origins on the street - the struggle for survival. The ice cream and noodle shops, the nail salons, they all emerged this way. Inside the Kwong King Building, there are many of these "combined shops" that cultural researchers love to study - they appear to have been together since ancient times, but only the shoe repair and key duplication shop, Cheng Shun, is the most classic example. Cheng Shun has become a research subject for countless university students, as physical keys have become almost obsolete in recent years, making Cheng Shun almost like a museum, supporting the new cultural economy of the Kwong King Building

老街坊首飾.玉器工作坊

2023年至現今

新界荃灣眾安街25號2005鋪

受訪者：區錫坤先生

業務性質：首飾玉器

你好！歡迎光臨老街坊玉石。

在荃灣眾安街街市的繁華中，我們猶如一顆閃爍的寶石。到今天，隨著玉石的稀缺，我們是你的一個玉石收容所和修補店，為那些破損的玉石提供一個新的生命。在玉石的光影下，這裡是街坊每日過來放下煩憂，欣賞玉石工藝。一個在街市的玉石博物館，除了展示玉石工藝和行業的變化，亦把寄存玉石的客人的故事讓大家細味，每個都是最少五代的故事。

猶記得第一次見面，陳小姐和媽媽把翠綠玉石交到區先生手上，說要為下個月的婚禮訂造一隻手鈪。轉眼，陳小姐變成了張太太，帶著手抱嬰孩過來，一邊抽著兩斤菜心，一邊把手鈪再放到工藝師手上，說要改造幾女兒的禮物。由一分二，變了親子玉石首飾。下次再見，眨眼應有20年，沒有見到張太太，由女兒把媽媽的耳環帶過來，希望變成一雙婚介。上星期，有位年輕女仕把婚介帶過來，問到有沒有辦法把介指改造成為襟針。原來爸爸早兩年過身了，媽媽下月登六，希望為媽媽做一份獨一禮物。

玉石，無限變化，修改，串連幾代人，成為家的一個記憶。

由玉石看家庭歷史和生活變遷，讓我們這顆在眾安街街市的寶石為你把家的故事寄存吧。

梅詩華

梅詩華 (Sarah)，香港中文大學和倫敦大學學院建築系畢業，現為香港註冊建築師，「一口設計工作室」和「一口舍群」的共同創辦人。Sarah 相信設計能夠發起行動、創造改變，因而致力把以人為本的設計思維融入協力創作當中，連繫大眾與社區空間、建立社區的凝聚力。

Old Neighbourhood Jade and Jewellery

2023 to present

Shop2005, 5 Chung On St, Tsuen Wan

Interviewee: Mr. Au Shek Kwan

Business: Jewelry and Jade

Hello! Welcome to the Old Neighbourhood Jade.

In this bustling Tsuen Wan Chung On Street Market, we are like a sparkling gem. Today, with the scarcity of jade, we are your jade shelter and repair shop, providing a new life for those damaged jades. Under the light and shadow of jade, this is where the neighbours come every day to put down their worries and appreciate jade craftsmanship. A jade living museum in the market, in addition to displaying the changes in jade craftsmanship and industry, also savours the stories of the guests who deposit their jades with us, each of which is at least five generations of family stories.

I can still remember the first meeting, Miss Chan and her mother handed the emerald jade to Mr. Au, saying that they wanted to order a bracelet for next month’s wedding. In the blink of an eye, Miss Chan became Mrs. Cheung, carrying a baby in her arms, pulling a bag of choy sum on one side, and putting the bracelet back into the hands of our craftsman, saying that she wanted to transform the bracelet into a gift for her baby daughter. From one to two, it became a parent-child jade jewellery. The next time we meet again, it should be a good 20 years. I haven’t seen Mrs. Cheung this time. The daughter brought her mother’s earrings and hoped to become a pair of wedding rings. Last week, a young lady brought the wedding ring pair and asked if there was any way to transform the rings into a brooch. It turned out that her father had passed away two years ago, and her mother was going to have her 60th birthday next month, hoping to make a lasting gift for her mother.

One piece of jade, infinite changes, modifications, connecting several generations, becoming a memory of home.

From jade to see family history and life changes, let our jade shop, this forever sparkling gem in the Chung On Street Market, keep your family story for you.

寶貝改衣專門店

2010年至現今

荃灣福來邨香車街街市地下32-42號

受訪者：邱師傅 (Gigi)

業務性質: 改衣

成長的不同階段，我們都經歷過「 not fit in」的時刻：S、M、L碼都不是太合身，卻沒有其他選擇。拉鍊壞了，該買新的會比找人縫補化算？有紀念價值的衣物，可是尺碼已不合，是否應該丟掉？

在服裝的層面上，我們或許暫時遵循標準化的「大眾」尺寸，但總會有一段「邊緣化」的時期，我們對衣服有獨特而重要的需求，卻又感到無助。

在「寶貝改衣專門店」，我們可以看到「獨特性」的專注和「修補咩都得」的技藝精神。店長Gigi最喜歡的工作之一就是修改婚紗。她說「只有合身的婚紗才能展現出美感，否則看起來會顯得臃腫。」，見到一個個待嫁的新娘子因穿上合身的婚紗，展現光芒和自信， Gigi都感到無限滿足。這證明服裝的一點改變，它所帶來稱心如意的影響是具大的。

在以利潤和效率為重的社會企業中，小店卻守著人的獨特性。縫補業賦予人們對「啱心水」的渴求以可能性，讓每個人都覺得自己是能夠自愛的「寶貝」。

服裝不僅僅是功能性的，它也能拯救我們當下的人生。

跌傷了腳，發現自己穿褲子變得困難; 老人常買到的雪紡衣物原來多半是人造纖維，密不透風，容易引起皮膚敏感; 需要整天使用尿袋的人，怎也不想把尿袋公開展示。然而，市面上的衣物卻只考慮到健全人士的需求。

「縫補寶」聘聘請本地婦女和退休人士，為康健服裝及週邊商品進行車縫，同時教育大眾正確的照顧方法，減輕長者和照顧者的負擔。我想像在「縫補寶」的工作間中，經常會有這樣的對話：「這樣做會不會更適合你呢？因為你…」「考慮一下車工的角度，這樣穿起來不會那麼辛苦」「這樣車縫，既隱藏了又美觀，你覺得如何？」這是一場思考別人困難的同時，也挑戰自己技藝的貢獻大賽。

這激發了我思考，以相同的思路改進我生活中的服裝，回應我的缺陷和需要，應該可以大大地減輕日常的自責情緒。例如，我非常健忘，是否可以訂制全由袋組成的衣服，方便放置物品？又或者，我有點S腰，可否加支針不時篤我，提醒要挻直腰骨？還有，可以替我選擇一種超有彈性的物料，製作一件既修身又適合上班的親子外套，女兒寒冷時又可以用外套扣著她，摟在懷中？此外，我們的工作是否只是為了糊口，還是一個熱情投入的契機？同樣地這也成為消費者支持信念的一個契機？

在服裝的美學設計和生產過程中，實際上關心著人與人之間所珍視的價值。每個參與其中的人都能在其中彰顯自己的聲音。單單看「寶貝改衣專門店」和「縫補寶」這種社會實踐已是一種價值的倡議，提出了「我們可以透過這種方式經營生意，建立一個多元的社會」的想像。

Baby Professional Tailor and Curtain Shop

2010 to present

Shop 32-42, Heung Che Street Market and Cooked Food Centre, 8 Tso Kung St, Tsuen Wan

Interviewee: Master Yau (Gigi)

Business: Sewing and Alterations

Growing up, we all experience moments of not fitting in: when sizes S, M, or L don’t quite match, yet there are no other options. A broken zipper—should you buy new or have it repaired? And what about cherished clothing with sizes that no longer fit? Should they be discarded?

On the clothing front, we may temporarily adhere to standardized “one-size-fits-all” measurements, but there’s always a period of feeling marginalized. We have unique and important clothing needs, yet often feel helpless.

Enter the “Baby Alteration Boutique,” where a focus on uniqueness and the art of repair prevails. Store owner Gigi’s favorite task is altering wedding gowns. She believes that only a well-fitting wedding dress truly showcases beauty; otherwise, it can appear bulky. Seeing each bride glow with confidence in a perfectly fitted gown brings immense satisfaction to Gigi. It proves that even small clothing changes can have a significant “gratifying” impact.

In a profit-driven world, this small boutique prioritizes individuality. The alteration business grants people the possibility of having clothes that truly suit them, making everyone feel like a cherished “baby.”

Clothing isn’t just functional; it can save our present life. Consider a sprained ankle making pants difficult to put on, elderly individuals unknowingly buying non-breathable synthetic fabrics that irritate their skin, or someone needing to discreetly wear a urine bag. Yet, mainstream clothing often overlooks these specific needs.

The “Sewing Home adaptive clothing” hires local women and retirees to mend health-conscious clothing and educate the public on proper care methods, easing burdens for seniors and caregivers. I imagine conversations in the “Sewing Home adaptive clothing” workspace: “Would this alteration suit you better? Considering your…”; “Think from the seamstress’s perspective—this way, it won’t be so uncomfortable to wear”; “This stitching hides imperfections and looks elegant—what do you think?” It’s a contest of empathy and skill, addressing others’ difficulties while challenging one’s own craftsmanship.

This inspires me this could improve my own wardrobe with a similar mindset—to address my flaws and needs, alleviating daily self-blame. Perhaps more all-made-of-pockets-clothing for my forgetfulness, occasional reminders to straighten my posture, or a flexible fabric for a tailored parent-child coat that keeps my daughter warm and close. And is our work merely for sustenance, or an opportunity for passionate engagement? This is also a chance for consumer to buy into the belief.

In the aesthetics and production of clothing, they genuinely care about the values cherished between people. Each participants during the process can express their voice. Just observing the “Baby Alteration Boutique” and “Sewing Home adaptive clothing” exemplifies a valuable initiative—one that envisions a diverse society through business practices.

Yim Siu Fong

YIM Sui Fong is an artist who specializes in video, sound and social practice art. She is a co-founder of Rooftop Institute and an assistant professor in the Fine Arts Department at The Chinese University of Hong Kong.
